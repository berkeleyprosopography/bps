  • November 23, 2015 — Society of Biblical Literature, Atlanta

    Laurie presented a paper Digital Tools Supporting Prosopographical Research in Texts and Manuscripts, in the section "Digital Humanities in Biblical, Early Jewish, and Christian Studies; Papyrology and Early Christian Backgrounds." This year's theme was Papyrology and Digital Humanities, chaired by BPS research partner Carrie Schroeder. In this talk, Laurie outlined the benefit of digital prosopographical tools for research in ancient text corpora, and more generally, the potential of BPS to contribute to the development of new research questions.
