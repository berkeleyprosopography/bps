Research IT

Our work would not have been possible without the ongoing support of Berkeley's Research IT unit. Our technical lead, Patrick Schmitz, has contributed his time and expertise from the outset. At the earliest stages of BPS development, Steven Masover participated in discussions that contributed to the interoperability and extensibility of BPS. Our thanks to director of Research IT David Greenbaum for building and supporting this wonderful team.

DH@Berkeley

  • DH@Berkeley staff members Quinn Dombrowski, Digital Humanities Coordinator, and Camille Villa, Digital Humanities Assistant (2014-2016), have generously offered unlimited support in all areas of BPS's development: designing mock-ups of user interfaces, supporting website development, and advising on a myriad of matters small and large.
  • DHSI 2015: In Berkeley's first Digital Humanities Summer Institute, Laurie Pearce and UC NES graduate student Eduardo Escobar received training in Gephi, an open-source visualization and exploration program. This provided them with the opportunity to consider how and whether aspects or components of this powerful tool could be integrated into the BPS visualization module.
